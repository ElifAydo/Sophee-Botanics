Glycerin - ✗ Not Used
A common humectant in natural skincare, but also a carbohydrate. We leave it out - always.

Formulated without glycerin - by design.

Glycerin is a sugar alcohol used in most natural moisturizers as a humectant. It draws water to the skin surface effectively - but it's also a carbohydrate that can absorb transdermally. For those following precise nutritional protocols, that matters. Every Sophee product is formulated without glycerin, sorbitol, cornstarch, or any carbohydrate-based ingredient.

Apply 3–5 drops to clean, dry skin morning and evening. Warm between palms before pressing gently into skin. Suitable for all skin types. Patch test recommended for reactive skin.
